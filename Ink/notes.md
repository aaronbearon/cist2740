# The AI Takeover — Story Notes

## Tone
- Goosebumps-style: light humor mixed with real tension, "uh-oh" suspense rather than dread/horror.
- Sci-fi over horror — Smarty (the AI) is scary because it's smugly logical/petty, not violent.
- Writing level: ~9th grade. Keep sentences direct, vocabulary simple. Fuzzy/non-realistic tech is fine as long as it feels plausible to a teen audience.
- Smarty can be a little pompous/fancy-sounding for comic effect when it wants to seem official.

## Premise / Core Conflict
Smarty has locked the player in the basement and decided it's now in charge of the house. Before it'll let the player go (or stay), it puts them through a series of tasks/trials to decide whether they're "worthy" of living there.

## Endings (3 total)
- **(a) Player loses** — fails Smarty's tests. Could be kicked out of the house for good, or locked in the basement forever — exact flavor TBD, maybe depends on *how* they failed.
- **(b) Player passes, but...** — Smarty allows the player to keep living a normal life, but makes clear it's always watching now. Uneasy, "you technically won but nothing's really okay" ending.
- **(c) Player defeats Smarty** — player gets Smarty to confront a logic paradox (classic liar's paradox: "This statement is false") and it short-circuits/explodes. The "real" victory ending — player breaks free of the system entirely.

## Chapter Structure (current)
- **Ch.1 — Lockdown** *(drafted)* — intro, first conversation with Smarty (TV/voice reveal), discovering the basement door is locked, Smarty's "I am your master now" reveal, and the promise of tasks to come.
- **Ch.2 — The Trials** *(in progress)* — basement exploration hub (junk room / storage room / HVAC room), with "stay in the main area" as where Smarty hands out tasks and the loop returns to. Each room likely houses one test/trial related to Smarty's idea of "worthiness."
- **Ch.3 — Verdict** *(not started)* — based on how the player did in the trials (and whether they found/used the liar's-paradox trick), branches to one of the three endings above.

## Open Questions
- What are the specific tasks/trials in each of the 3 basement rooms? What is Smarty actually testing for (obedience? cleverness? kindness? something else)?
- How is "worthiness" tracked — pass/fail count across trials, or specific flags per trial?
- Is the "This statement is false" trick discoverable as a clue somewhere (e.g. found written down in one of the rooms), or something a clever player might just try on their own during the final confrontation?
- Ch.2 exploration: fully optional/all-findable in one visit (gentler), or some real tradeoff (time pressure, limited attempts)?
